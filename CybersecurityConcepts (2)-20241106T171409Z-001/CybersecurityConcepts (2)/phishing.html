Phishing

Phishing is a cyberattack that tricks people into sharing sensitive data or downloading harmful software. It's a type

of social engineering that targets human errors. Phishing attacks often use fake emails, texts, or websites to convince users

to reveal personal information like passwords or financial details.

Types of Phishing Attacks

Bulk Email Phishing: Attackers send fake emails to large groups of people, hoping that a few will respond. These emails

often appear to be from well-known brands to look legitimate.

Spear Phishing: This is a targeted phishing attack that focuses on a specific individual or group. The attacker uses

personal information about the target to make the message seem more credible. For example, they might pose as a school principal

in an email asking for personal information to qualify for an award.

Smishing and Vishing: Smishing uses SMS messages, and vishing involves voice calls to trick victims. Scammers might pose

as a bank or government agency to request financial details over the phone or text.

How to Recognize Phishing Attempts

Phishing scams often use certain tactics to create urgency or appeal to emotions. Some warning signs include:

Suspicious URLs: Fake URLs may look similar to real websites but often have small differences, like extra letters or misspellings.

Urgent Language: Messages might pressure you to act immediately, like "Update your account now!" or "Your account will be suspended."

Requests for Sensitive Data: Legitimate companies rarely ask for personal or financial information through email or text. If unsure, contact the company directly.
