Passwords

Passwords are crucial when it comes to protecting online accounts and sensitive information. Strong, unique passwords

are essential for keeping hackers out of your personal data. Here are some guidelines for creating effective passwords:

Characteristics of a Strong Password

Long: Passwords should be at least 12 characters. The longer the password, the harder it is for hackers to guess.

Unique: Each account should have its own password to prevent a single data breach from compromising multiple accounts.

Avoid reusing passwords, even with minor differences.

Complex: Use a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.

Changing Passwords:

Regularly changing your passwords will probably be unnessary if your password is already strong,

unless you suspect your password has been compromised.

Password Management

Managing your passwords can often be difficult. Using a password manager can offer you a secure way to store and manage

passwords, so you only need to remember one master password. This helps reduce potential of being hacked because of password reuse.
