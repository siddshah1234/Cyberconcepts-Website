Potential Threats

Cyber threats are constantly evolving as attackers develop new methods to breach security

and exploit vulnerabilities. Here are some of the most common types of cyber threats organizations and individuals face:

Malware

Malware is designed to damage or disrupt systems. It can compromise the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.

Common types include viruses, spyware, and trojans. Malware can steal information or damage files and software.

Ransomware

Ransomware encrypts a victim's data, making it inaccessible until a ransom is paid. This type of malware has become increasingly

popular due to its high financial return for attackers. Preventing ransomware attacks requires regular data backups, employee training,

and strong security protocols.

Distributed Denial of Service Attacks

DDoS attacks flood a target's network with excessive traffic, overwhelming systems and making online services unavailable.

These attacks often serve as distractions for other types of cyber attacks and can be very disruptive for organizations, especially

those relying heavily on online services.

Corporate Account Takeover

Corporate Account Takeover involves cybercriminals gaining unauthorized access to a business's financial accounts.

Attackers can commit fraudulent transactions during Corporate Account Takeover. Businesses with weak security measures

are particularly vulnerable to this type of attack.

ATM Cash-Out

ATM Cash-Out is a type of fraud where attackers use stolen card information to make large, unauthorized cash withdrawals from ATMs.

They may change ATM settings to allow "Unlimited Operations," resulting in large financial losses for banks and other financial institutions.

Protecting Against Cyber Threats

Organizations and individuals can reduce cyber risks by implementing strong security measures, such as updating software, limiting access to

sensitive data, and educating employees about cybersecurity best practices. Being aware of these threats and taking steps can help

defend against potential attacks.
